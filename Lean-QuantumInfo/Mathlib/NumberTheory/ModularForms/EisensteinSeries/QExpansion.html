    theorem EisensteinSeries.qExpansion_identity {k : ℕ} (hk : 1 ≤ k) (z : UpperHalfPlane) :
    ∑' (n : ℤ), 1 / (↑z + ↑n) ^ (k + 1) = (-2 * ↑Real.pi * Complex.I) ^ (k + 1) / ↑k.factorial * ∑' (n : ℕ), ↑n ^ k * Complex.exp (2 * ↑Real.pi * Complex.I * ↑z) ^ n

    This is one key identity relating infinite series to q-expansions which shows that ∑' n, 1 / (z + n) ^ (k + 1) = ((-2 π I) ^ (k + 1) / k !) * ∑' n, n ^ k q ^n where q = cexp (2 π I z).
